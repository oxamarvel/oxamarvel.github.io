Identity Constructed

A personal 3D introduction project where my name, OXA, is constructed inside an industrial, bunker-like environment.

Each letter assembled in a distinct system, allowing me to showcase different technical and artistic approaches in a single cohesive scene.

Industrial Infrastructure

Materials are created procedurally using shader nodes to ensure flexibility and consistency across the environment.

Concrete surfaces feature roughness variation and cracking, while pipes use layered shaders to blend peeled paint and rust, suggesting long-term use, exposure, and structural aging.

Atmosphere & Lightning

Volumetric dust fills the space to enhance depth and scale, reinforcing the heavy, enclosed atmosphere of an underground facility.

Lighting is integrated directly into the environment using cylindrical mesh lamps with emission shaders, creating controlled and artificial illumination typical of industrial spaces.

O — Physics & Momentum

The letter O is treated as a heavy circular object and animated using rigid body simulation. Its motion is driven by gravity and mass as it rolls down a ramp, emphasizing inertia and weight.

A simple guiding plane secures the rolling path and prevents unwanted toppling, preserving stable yet natural physical behavior.

X — Controlled Motion

The letter X rises vertically from a bottom hatch using keyframed translation with eased timing. Unlike the physically driven motion of the O, this movement is fully controlled, representing a deliberate system activation.

The restrained motion emphasizes precision and introduces contrast between simulation-based behavior and mechanically programmed movement.

A — Mechanical Interaction

The letter A is assembled and positioned by a robotic mechanism designed to feel functional and mechanically believable.

The robot arm is driven by an inverse-kinematics rig, allowing coordinated motion. The gripping hand uses rigid body simulation to physically interact with the letter, while piston movement is regulated using damped track constraints.

Reflection & Learnings

Key Learnings

  • Rigid body stability improves with guiding geometry and controlled system handoffs.
  • IK rigs combined with damped constraints help achieve believable mechanical behavior.

Future Improvements

  • Introduce more secondary motion (vibration, recoil, settling) to enhance perceived mechanical weight.
  • Expand environmental detail with additional props and clutter to increase immersion.
