Art direction and asset production for a 3-day game jam submission, emphasizing minimalist visuals and modular vector asset design.

Project Overview

The Score Jam #37 challenged participants to create a game that implements a score/leaderboard system within 3 days, with an additional theme: Soccer.

Score Up! is a fast-paced arcade game inspired by keepy-uppy, developed in Unity. Given a limited countdown, players move horizontally and kick in both directions, maintaining the ball from touching the ground. Score is gained by successfully kicking the ball above the character’s head, encouraging precision and rhythm.

Thematic Inspiration

Keepy-uppy is commonly used as a presentation test for new footballers joining a team, often performed on a stage in front of sponsors, media, and the public.

This concept inspired the theme direction to frame the gameplay as a public performance—a stage with sponsor branding, journalist silhouettes surrounding the play area with occasional camera flashes, and a camera viewfinder overlay framing the player “on record”.

These elements reinforce pressure without interrupting gameplay.

Art Style Direction

A minimalist vector art style with vibrant colors was selected to support fast production and iteration within the game jam timeframe:

  • Flat shapes and clean silhouettes ensure visual clarity.
  • High-saturation elements highlight active gameplay objects.
  • Background and stage use muted tones to reduce visual noise and keep focus on the ball and player.

This balance maintains clarity while preserving energy.

Asset Production & Pipeline

All assets were created in CorelDRAW and were separated intentionally to support rotational and pin-based movement (e.g., foot rotation) in the Unity engine.

This modular setup enabled faster iteration, easier animation control, and cleaner collaboration with the programmer.

Reflection & Learnings

Key Learnings

  • Modular assets simplify animation and adjustments.
  • Environmental cues can replace complex storytelling.
  • Early art–code alignment is critical under tight deadlines.

Future Improvements

  • Athlete’s idle animation.
  • Score-reactive audience or journalist silhouettes.
  • Combo-based lighting or screen feedback.
