Art direction and enemy asset production for a 7-day game jam submission themed around eldritch descent, exploring pixel art aesthetics and a 3D-to-2D asset production pipeline.

Project Overview

The Eye Jam challenged participants to create a game that explores eldritch/cosmic horror themes within 7 days, with an additional theme: Descent.

ChandraSekhar is inspired by the cosmic phenomenon of binary star merging. Developed in Unity, the player controls Chandra, a sentient star descending through hostile space to find his pair, Sekhar. The journey is obstructed by corrupted stellar entities that attempt to pull Chandra off course using their gravitational forces, forming a bullet-hell survival experience.

Thematic Inspiration

To interpret the eldritch theme, familiar cosmic objects were distorted with uncanny biological traits.

Motifs such as watching eyes, pulsing flesh, tentacles, and unnatural anatomy were embedded into enemy star designs, transforming astronomical bodies into sentient, unsettling beings that constantly observe and threaten the player.

Art Style Direction

Pixel art was chosen for its clarity and abstraction. Simplified forms and strong silhouettes maintain visibility in bullet-hell gameplay, while the implied detail enhances the uncanny, cosmic tone of the world.

Each star is defined by a distinct hue to ensure immediate recognition. Colors remain vibrant yet subdued, balancing visual separation with an oppressive cosmic atmosphere.

Enemy Concept

The goal of the enemy concept was to preserve scientific recognizability while injecting unease through biological distortion:

  • White dwarf star—seraphim-like entity, false divinity.
  • Brown dwarf star—tentacular mass, incomplete birth.
  • Red giant star—pulsing mass of flesh.
  • Neutron star—dense star with a devouring mouth.
  • Black hole—the watching void, awareness beyond form.

This balance maintains clarity while preserving energy.

Enemy Asset Production Workflow

The base model for enemies were created and animated in Blender for fast iteration, but still maintaining organic motion and consistent lighting across frames.

Rendered outputs were processed in CorelDRAW to scale down asset size and adjust the color palette. Anti-aliasing was intentionally disabled on export to preserve crisp pixel edges.

Final pixel cleanup and visual polish were completed in Aseprite.

Reflection & Learnings

Key Learnings

  • Strong silhouettes are critical for visual clarity in bullet-hell games.
  • Limited animation frames can still convey life with intentional motion.
  • 3D workflows can accelerate complex pixel asset production.

Future Improvements

  • More dynamic visual progression tied to descent depth.
  • Subtle screen or sprite distortion effects for eldritch emphasis.
