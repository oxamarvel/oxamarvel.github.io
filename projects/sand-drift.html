SAND DRIFT

  • YEAR2023
  • ROLE3D GENERALIST

A personal study of procedural terrain workflows, rigid body vehicle simulation, and cinematography in Blender.

Project Intent & Inspiration

Sand Drift is a personal 3D project exploring cinematic vehicle motion in a vast desert environment.

The goal was to recreate the visual language of automotive advertisements—emphasizing scale, power, and controlled chaos through dust, motion, and lighting.

Procedural Dune Landscape

The dune environment was built using procedural geometry, implementing noise-based height variation to form large-scale mountain ranges.

This non-destructive approach allowed rapid iteration and flexible scale adjustments while maintaining natural variation and believable erosion patterns.

Procedural Sandstone Shading

All terrain materials, including sandstone mountains and sand surfaces, were created using procedural shaders.

Layered noise and displacement were used to avoid image textures, ensuring consistency across scale while preserving natural surface variation.

Volumetric Dust

To enhance atmospheric ambience, volumetric dust was introduced to simulate wind-blown sand commonly found in desert environments.

Density and movement were driven by noise and directional controls, carefully tuned to avoid overpowering the vehicle’s silhouette.

Subdivision Modeling & Motion Through Physics

The vehicle was inspired by off-road car designs and modeled in Blender using subdivision techniques.

Instead of manual keyframing, vehicle motion was driven using rigid body simulation to achieve natural weight transfer and responsive suspension behavior.

Lighting & Camera Direction

Natural lighting was introduced to establish a realistic desert mood and strong form definition.

Camera direction was designed to follow automotive advertisement conventions, using multiple angles, longer lenses, and controlled highlights to emphasize motion and silhouette.

Reflection & Learnings

Key Learnings

  • Procedural workflows significantly improve iteration speed and flexibility.
  • Physics-based motion adds subtle realism that is difficult to achieve through manual animation.

Future Improvements

  • Add texture painting and advanced shading to introduce wear, dust, scratches, and surface detail.
  • Implement a particle-based dust trail system for stronger wheel–terrain interaction.
