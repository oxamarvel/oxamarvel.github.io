KOPLING

  • YEAR2025
  • ROLEGAME DESIGNER

Game design documentation for a cozy, narrative-driven simulation game developed in Unity.

Kopling was developed as a final project at Apple Developer Academy. The initial concept originated from an observation of foreign interest in the "kopi keliling" business culture, highlighting its uniqueness as a social and cultural phenomenon.

Powered by Unity and developed on Mac, Kopling lets players step into the shoes of a kopling brewer. The game blends light simulation and narrative storytelling, creating a grounded yet cozy experience. Other than making drinks, players are encouraged to slow down, observe, and empathize — echoing the real warmth of alternate Jakarta’s streets and the quiet companionship between strangers.

The game invites introspection, asking players to notice the humanity behind every customer and the stories hidden behind each cup.

Game Design Research & References

Audience research indicated that approximately 1.6 million players prefer narrative-driven experiences, reinforcing the decision to prioritize story over complex mechanics.

The design draws inspiration from successful narrative-focused games such as:

  • VA-11 HALL-A (~1M copies sold, est. gross $12M)
  • Coffee Talk (~600K copies sold, est. gross $4.2M)

This type of games are very popular because of low skill barrier to entry, appeals to highly engaged niche audiences, and very community-shareable (TikTok/YouTube).

Core Gameplay Loop

The design focuses on comfort, intimacy, and emotional presence rather than challenge or mastery.

The Gameplay Structure

  • A customer approaches and requests a specific drink.
  • The player prepares the drink through simple click-and-drag interactions, following familiar brewing steps commonly found on sachet drink packaging.
  • Successful preparation unlocks a conversation phase.
  • Dialogue choices appear at key moments.
  • Player responses influence narrative direction and branching outcomes.

The simplicity of the system was intentional—reducing cognitive load during gameplay to ensure the player’s focus remains on narrative engagement rather than mechanical difficulty.

Narrative Design

The narrative centers on topics commonly experienced by middle- and lower-income communities—the primary customer of kopi keliling. Conversations touch on everyday concerns such as employment, financial pressure, family, and politics, framing these issues through casual, personal dialogue rather than overt commentary.

Narrative progression uses branching dialogue, where key conversation moments present multiple response options. Player choices influence relationship direction and story outcomes, allowing each interaction to diverge into different narrative paths without enforcing explicit success or failure states.

Dialogue and branching logic were implemented directly through CSV-based conversation files, enabling structured control over dialogue flow, conditional responses, and narrative variations while supporting rapid iteration during development.

Reflection & Learnings

Key Learnings

  • Simple mechanics can effectively support narrative depth when cognitive load is kept low.
  • Branching dialogue increases player agency even without complex systemic consequences.
  • Grounding narrative themes in real social contexts strengthens emotional engagement.

Future Implementation

  • Expand branching depth with longer-term consequence tracking across multiple encounters.
  • Introduce lightweight relationship variables to subtly influence dialogue outcomes.
  • Improve tooling for narrative visualization to better map branching complexity.
