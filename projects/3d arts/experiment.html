Sand Drift

Terrain-driven vehicle motion study using physics-based interaction

Project Overview

Sand Drift explores how terrain topology influences vehicle motion and player perception. The project focuses on simulating surface resistance, directional slip, and momentum decay across uneven sand geometry.

Process & Technical Approach

Terrain meshes were sculpted with exaggerated height variation to amplify suspension response. Vehicle movement was driven by surface-normal sampling combined with velocity damping, enabling slope-aware acceleration and drift.

Visual feedback was reinforced using shader-based sand deformation and directional particle emission to communicate traction loss and momentum transfer.
